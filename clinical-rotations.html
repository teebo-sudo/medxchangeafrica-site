Clinical Rotations · Always supervised

Supervised clinical rotations at partner hospitals in Ghana.

Rotate through the wards at Cape Coast Teaching Hospital and Elmina Government Hospital as part of the professional team led by Dr Ernest K. Ainooson, with hands-on exposure to tropical medicine and public-health realities you'll rarely meet at home. You observe, you assist, you grow — always within your scope and always supervised.

Part of the team

Learning alongside the people who lead the ward.

On rotation you join the daily rhythm of the wards — morning rounds, outpatient clinics and bedside teaching — as part of Dr Ainooson's resident team. The local clinicians are the experts and the leaders; you are here to learn from how they work, and to contribute within your training.

A word on scope

Hands-on always means supervised, and always within your training.

Local clinicians lead every rotation. You learn by observing and assisting under their direction — never by working unsupervised or beyond your competence. This is what keeps the exchange safe for patients and meaningful for you, and it is non-negotiable.

02Partner hospitals

Two teaching hospitals, one stretch of coast.

Teaching hospital · Cape Coast

Cape Coast Teaching Hospital

The regional referral and teaching hospital — a broad case mix across medicine, surgery, paediatrics and maternal health, and the heart of most rotations under Dr Ainooson's team.

District hospital · Elmina

Elmina Government Hospital

A busy district hospital where you see frontline community care up close — closer to the day-to-day realities of public health on the coast.

03Public-health highlight

Once a month, the wards come to the village.

Once a month the team runs a free health-screening clinic in nearby villages — blood-pressure and blood-sugar checks, basic consultations and health education, brought to people who would otherwise travel far for them. For students it is one of the clearest views of public health and preventive care on the coast, and a chance to work as part of the wider team.

Like the rest of the program, the Free Clinic is led by local clinicians and built on partnership and mutual learning — never charity.
